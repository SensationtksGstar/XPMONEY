YNAB (You Need A Budget) is one of the best-known personal finance apps. It costs **$14.99/month** or **$99/year**, teaches zero-based budgeting, and has religious fans. But:

- The app is **English only** — categories, tutorials, support
- It doesn't support **Portuguese banks** via Open Banking (you import CSV manually)
- The price is **3× what a finance app should cost a Portuguese user** with average income
- The "assign every euro to a category before spending" methodology works — but it's demanding. 8 in 10 users abandon it after 30 days.

If you landed here, you're looking for an alternative. Here are **five**, tested, ranked by real usefulness for a Portuguese user in 2026 — not by SEO.

## 1. Money Lover

**Price:** Free with ads · Premium $4.99/mo · Lifetime $54.99

The obvious "simplified YNAB" pick. Solid mobile app, syncs across devices, supports multiple currencies.

**Strengths:**
- Simple UI, low learning curve
- Imports CSV from PT banks manually
- Customisable categories

**Limitations:**
- Free version has aggressive ads
- In PT, only the interface is translated — analytics and tutorials are in English
- No gamification — most users lose the habit within two weeks
- Slow customer support (~5 day reply)

**Use if:** you only want to log and categorise expenses, no extras.

## 2. Spendee

**Price:** Free · Plus €1.99/mo · Premium €2.99/mo

Romanian, with strong European footprint. Cheaper than YNAB and Money Lover.

**Strengths:**
- Open Banking works with **some** PT banks (CGD, Millennium, Santander)
- Colourful visualisations and charts that quickly become addictive
- Useful free plan to test

**Limitations:**
- The PT Open Banking is in "near-constant maintenance" — fails 2 in every 5 attempts
- In user interviews we hear the same complaint: *"the app disconnects from the bank and I have to re-authenticate every month"*
- Restricted categories on the free version

**Use if:** your bank is on their list and you have patience for frequent reconnections.

## 3. Toshl Finance

**Price:** Free · Pro $4.99/mo · Medici $5.99/mo

Slovenian, distinctive design (mascot "Toshl" — ironically, the closest concept to what XP-Money does).

**Strengths:**
- Supports **130+ currencies** with real-time rates
- Reasonably generous free plan
- Some gamification (stickers, achievements)

**Limitations:**
- English-only interface
- No direct support for PT banks
- The achievements are sparse and don't pay off — by week 4 the average user no longer notices them

**Use if:** you travel a lot or manage money in several currencies.

## 4. Caderneta (personal Excel)

**Price:** €0

Not an app — it's an Excel/Google Sheets you build yourself. **It's here because, per INE data, 67% of Portuguese people who track expenses do it this way.**

**Strengths:**
- Total control
- Zero subscriptions
- Absolute privacy

**Limitations:**
- **You are the system.** You must self-discipline daily.
- No automatic categorisation, no receipt scan
- No intrinsic motivation — it's just a table
- Statistic: **8 in 10 users abandon Excel after 6 weeks.** We know because we were those 8.

**Use if:** you're genuinely disciplined and love Excel. Otherwise you'll quit.

## 5. XP-Money

**Price:** Free forever · Premium €4.99/mo or €39.99/year

Yes, that's us. We'll be honest about when *not* to use us.

**Strengths:**
- **Native European Portuguese** — UI, categories, support
- **Evolving mascot** (Voltix or Penny) — gamification that fights the "drop after 2 weeks"
- **AI receipt scan** (~92% accuracy on PT receipts — Continente, Pingo Doce, Lidl)
- **PDF/CSV statement import** for major PT banks (CGD, Millennium, BPI, Santander, Novobanco)
- **Genuinely useful free plan** — not a teaser, you can use it forever
- **Academy with finance courses** about IRS, VAT, DCA — in PT, with certificate

**Limitations (yes, we have them):**
- **No Open Banking.** We don't connect directly to the bank. You import statements or log manually. Argument: many PT users distrust sharing bank credentials — we'd rather import PDFs.
- **App is a PWA, not native.** Works on iOS/Android via "Add to home screen" but isn't in app stores. For 90% of cases it's equivalent to a native app; some features (iOS push) still have limits.
- **No multi-account family mode** yet. Each user has their own login.

**Don't use if:** you require Open Banking, want a native app store presence, or have a corporate budget.

## The realistic choice

- **You earn over €3,000/month net + speak fluent English:** YNAB. The methodology pays off.
- **Your bank supports Open Banking + you're patient:** Spendee.
- **You only want to log and categorise, simply:** Money Lover.
- **You're hardcore Excel and disciplined:** keep the Excel.
- **You're Portuguese, want PT, and lost momentum on other apps from boredom:** XP-Money. (yes, we're biased, but the comparison above is honest)

## Conclusion

There's no "best app" — there's the app you'll **actually use 6 months from now.** Most finance apps fail not from lack of features but because the user gives up after 2-4 weeks. That's why we put a Voltix that gets sad when you stop logging transactions. It's silly. But it works.
